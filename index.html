Hello! My name is Soulémane TAKPARA. I’m from Benin and I live in Parakou, BENIN . I studied sociology and anthropology a field of the humanities that focuses on society—and earned my bachelor’s degree in 2024. Even before that, I’d been passionate about web development since my freshman year of college. But due to a lack of financial resources, my parents couldn’t afford to enroll me in a technical school. So I waited until my sophomore year of college to buy a computer with the funds from my college scholarship. I began teaching myself using the lessons provided by a friend I had met at my university’s technical school. I kept improving and never gave up, despite the lack of a supportive environment to push me forward. Then, in 2025, I was lucky enough to be selected from among 150 young people to participate in a six-month web development training program. It was a dream come true. I took the program seriously and learned a great deal throughout it. And now this opportunity has presented itself to me as I’m about to talk about BYU. I’m very happy to be part of this program, and I’ll give it my all to learn as much as possible.

BENIN

Benin is a French-speaking country in West Africa. It is the birthplace of the Voodoo religion, as well as the territory of the former Kingdom of Dahomey (approximately 1600–1900). In Abomey, the former capital of Dahomey, the historical museum is housed in two royal palaces adorned with bas-reliefs depicting the kingdom’s history and a throne mounted on human skulls. To the north, Pendjari National Park offers safaris to spot elephants, hippos, and lions.
